**Version 2026.3.1 - Global Game Update**

---

### **Bug Fixes & Security Updates**

- **South Korean Police Wallet Glitch**: Fixed a critical bug where South Korean police accidentally leaked the wallet password for seized cryptocurrency, resulting in a loss of $5M. The wallet security has been updated to include unguessable passwords like 'hunter2'. Apologies have been issued to the player base for this preventable exploit. 

- **ISP Monopoly Expansion**: Charter has been granted permission to merge with Cox, effectively becoming the largest ISP in the US. Despite protests, the FCC has decided there's no reason to worry about higher internet prices because, apparently, competition doesn't matter in this game. Players in non-competitive regions might experience a slight lag increase due to 'service integration'.

### **New Features**

- **Iowa Data Center Zoning**: Implemented strict zoning rules for data centers in Iowa. While these rules are among the strictest, local residents still feel the game balance is off. We're monitoring player feedback for potential future patches.

- **iPad Air Refresh**: The M4 iPad Air now features a significant RAM boost to 12GB. Other than that, it's pretty much the same old iPad Air with a new chip. Enjoy the smoother multitasking, players!

- **iPhone 17e**: Introducing the iPhone 17e with an A19 chip, MagSafe, and 256GB storage at $599. This basic model replaces last year's iPhone 16e, maintaining the same price point. It's like we're recycling, but with tech!

- **Subaru Trailseeker**: Added the 2026 Subaru Trailseeker, an electric vehicle with a nod to Subaru's heritage despite its Toyota platform. Players can now enjoy a more 'quintessentially Subaru' driving experience in the game.

- **AMD Ryzen AI 400**: New Ryzen AI 400 chips bring upgraded CPUs, GPUs, and NPUs to AM5 desktops, focusing on business PCs. DIY enthusiasts, we haven't forgotten you; keep an eye out for future updates.

### **Gameplay Adjustments**

- **SpaceX Funding Limiter**: A new law proposed by a former NASA chief turned lobbyist aims to cap SpaceX funding, promoting competition in the space race. This might slow down SpaceX's progression in the game but aims to keep the space industry balanced.

- **FCC Talk Radio Exemption**: Adjusted the enforcement of the equal-time rule to not apply to talk radio, because why should fairness apply everywhere? This change might affect how political discourse unfolds in the game.

### **World Events**

- **UK's Non-Intervention Policy**: Updated UK's foreign policy stance where PM Starmer has publicly criticized Trump's approach to regime change, emphasizing a 'no skies' policy. This might affect diplomatic relations and mission outcomes in the Middle East region.

- **Iran Leadership Strategy**: A new questline has been added where players must navigate the aftermath of a strategy to eliminate Iran's leadership. Security analysts provide in-game lore on what might happen next.

- **Dubai's Safe Haven Status**: Dubai's reputation as a safe haven has been altered following missile attacks. This change affects the city's attractiveness as a player destination, introducing new risks and challenges.

- **Middle East Conflict Map**: Added dynamic maps showing real-time strikes across Iran and the Middle East. Players can now track the progress of the US-Israel operations and their impact on the game world.

- **Asylum System Overhaul**: Changed the asylum system where refugee status is now temporary, lasting only 30 months. This adjustment introduces a time-sensitive element to the refugee questline.

- **Brewdog Acquisition**: Tilray has acquired Brewdog for £33m, leading to in-game events where bars close and jobs are lost. Players might find new quests related to the cannabis and beverage industry.
