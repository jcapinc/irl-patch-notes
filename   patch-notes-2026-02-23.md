**Patch Notes: Reality Update 2026.02**

---

### **General Updates**

- **Microsoft Gaming Chief Update**: New patch introduces Asha Sharma as the head of Microsoft Gaming. Sharma has implemented a "No Tolerance for Bad AI" policy, ensuring all AI within gaming ecosystems must now pass a rigorous 'Good AI' certification. However, players have noted Sharma's character stats are low in 'Gaming Experience', leading to community skepticism. 

- **Mazda CX-5 Update**: The 2026 Mazda CX-5 has received a size increase and a radical tech upgrade. Starting price set at $29,990 in-game currency. New features include larger cargo space and enhanced navigation systems, but beware of the 'Not Perfect' bug which might occasionally cause minor gameplay annoyances.

- **AI Training Data Patch**: AI entities now have the ability to generate near-verbatim copies of novels from training data, leading to a significant increase in the 'Memorization' skill. This update has sparked debates on AI ethics within the community forums.

- **Westeros Expansion**: 'Knight of the Seven Kingdoms' DLC released, bringing back the beloved Westeros magic. This prequel series has improved storytelling mechanics, reminding players why they fell in love with the original 'Game of Thrones' campaign.

- **Autonomous Vehicle Milestone**: New historical content added with the introduction of Quevedo's telekino from 1904, marking the first step towards fully autonomous vehicles like the Waymo. Players can now explore this evolution in the 'Road to Autonomy' questline.

### **Crime and Legal System**

- **Lord Mandelson Arrest**: A new quest has been added where players can follow the storyline of Lord Mandelson's arrest on suspicion of misconduct in public office. This questline includes interactive elements like police interviews in Camden.

- **Mexico Military Deployment**: Following the death of a drug lord, Mexico has deployed thousands of soldiers in-game to quell the violence. Players can now engage in missions to help stabilize regions or join the cartel's resistance for a different gameplay experience.

- **Nottingham Inquiry**: New content regarding the Nottingham killer, Valdo Calocane, who watched shooting videos online. This patch adds depth to the investigation mechanics, allowing players to analyze digital footprints.

- **BBC Apology**: During the Baftas event in-game, a racial slur was accidentally broadcast, leading to an immediate patch for content moderation. The BBC has issued an in-game apology, enhancing the realism of live event mishaps.

- **Nick Reiner Trial**: A new courtroom drama quest where players can participate in the trial of Nick Reiner, accused of killing his parents. Choose to defend or prosecute for different narrative outcomes.

- **Rape Suspect Escape**: A bug allowed a rape suspect to escape from jail prematurely. This has been patched, but the suspect is now an NPC abroad, adding an international element to the pursuit quest.

### **Cultural and Entertainment**

- **Royal Tea Party**: Queen Camilla has met with Gisèle Pelicot, leaving her 'speechless' over tea. This event is now a mini-game where players can engage in royal etiquette challenges.

- **Brit Awards**: PinkPantheress has won the Brit Award for best producer, becoming the first woman since 1977. This achievement unlocks new music production quests with female-led narratives.

- **Alan Carr's Castle**: Comedian Alan Carr has purchased a castle in the Scottish Borders, adding a new property to explore in the 'Celebrity Real Estate' expansion. Features 17 bedrooms and a working railway, providing unique exploration and management gameplay.

### **Sports and Fitness**

- **Vonn's Olympics Crash**: Lindsey Vonn's near leg loss from an Olympic crash is now a survival story quest, where players can experience the intensity of high-stakes sports injuries and recovery.

- **Boxing Rematch**: Manny Pacquiao and Floyd Mayweather are set for a professional rematch. Players can simulate this event, choosing strategies and outcomes, affecting the boxers' career stats.

- **Football Highlights**: Manchester United vs. Everton match now available with highlights. Benjamin Sesko's goal as a super-sub adds a dynamic to the substitution strategy in football management games.

- **Rugby Union**: England's Six Nations campaign has been updated to reflect their slide, offering players new challenges to identify and fix the 'Five Reasons' for their downturn.

- **Cricket Crisis**: Italian cricket faces a crisis with a sexual assault allegation. This introduces new ethical dilemmas and governance challenges in the cricket management simulation.

### **Technology and Environment**

- **AI Resignation Wave**: New narrative content where AI researchers resign from companies, leading to a questline about ethical AI development. Also, bots now hire humans, reversing traditional roles in some gameplay scenarios.

- **Battery Boom**: Despite political resistance, the US has seen a 'Big Battery Boom'. Players can now invest in or sabotage renewable energy projects, affecting the in-game environment.

- **Luxury EV Market**: Lamborghini has pulled out of luxury EVs due to low demand, impacting the luxury car market dynamics in the game. Players might see similar moves from other high-end brands.

- **Gear Updates**: New gear items added:
  - **Duffel Bags**: Eastpak Duffel Pack S Tarp Black2 is now the top pick for adventurers, enhancing storage capacity in travel scenarios.
  - **Puffer Jackets**: New jackets from Patagonia, Arc’teryx, and REI provide warmth and style, crucial for survival in cold environments.
